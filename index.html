Global impact of fast fashion

Fast fashion not only impacts the enviornment, but has a global impact as well! Some of our favourite brands (Nike, H&M,ZARA, etc.) are involved in huge scandals revealing their part in the fast fashion issue- depletion of natural resources and social issues as clothing gets cheaper and wardrobes are frequently updated. After female workers charged Nike for sexual assault, claims by the International Labour Rights Forum about bad working conditions, animal rights issues (exotic animal skin, hair, etc.) and the greenwashed case against them, Nike is making a start to improve their brand. Other brands have also sworn to stop sweatshops and child labour. Support these brands! Start researching into what YOU wear

Clothing production and disposal have serious environmental consequences, contributing to carbon emissions, water waste, and pollution. Synthetic fabrics like polyester release microplastics into waterways, harming marine life. cotton farming is water-intensive and involves harmful pesticides. Fast fashion's rapid turnover leads to massive textile waste, much of which ends up in landfills. This cycle not only depletes resources but also exploits workers in countries with weak labor laws. The need for sustainable practices is clear to reduce the industry's environmental impact.

Global Impact

Fast fashion has a profound global impact, particularly through the exploitation of workers, including child labor, in low-wage countries. The industry's demand for cheap labor often results in unsafe working conditions and violations of human rights. Additionally, the relentless pace of production leads to economic instability in developing regions, as local economies become dependent on exploitative practices. The negative social and ethical consequences ripple worldwide, highlighting the urgent need for fair labor practices and a more equitable global fashion industry.
